attention marketplace

insight

if all tokens are priced on attention in some sense, why not embrace that weakness and make it a strength? all coins are a proxy of mindshare. bitcoin, digital gold. pump, memes. hyperliquid, perps. and so on. no matter what the fundamentals to weigh stock, crypto, commodities or memes are — they're all priced beyond fundamentals (and) utility. the "beyond" is usually attention / urgency and narrative.

why this

distribution is the only moat and software increases by an order of magnitude. this is a line you've read in a million tweets. and that's true. 11 products will look the same, and the one with highest distribution/virality will likely win. and speculation can hijack attention. if all coins are priced on attention anyway, why not index on it?

how it works
  • launch coins at $125k mcap
  • teams hold 0 coins (so do investors)
  • the fundamental reasoning of the coin: bring attention to the product & business

prediction markets for startups

insight

speculation is net +ve for businesses. there's a multi-billion opportunity there. think acquired, angelist, polymarket and so on. but speculation comes in many forms.

tokens and their caveats
  • if they fall 80%, your odds of raising are hurt. and it will fall 80% because: capital allocation is important, narratives (and liquidity) shifts. macro tailwinds affect things
  • a perpetual token is good; you IPO / people can speculate. but 90% of the times it's a liability. "pump the chart" is the request, founders have to do things to increase price (instead of focusing on the product)
prediction markets

monthly markets on companies. they decide targets: increase MRR by 40%. hire 3 employees. launch XYZ product. scale XYZ to 10,000 users. any KPI is fair game. any. as long as you tell the current status (and) the goal. limited time speculation, doesn't peg your company's mcap but STILL, gives distribution enough (if done well, if you're a noisy founder it would work).

target users

this could 100% convince a YC or a16z batch or solo-app-devs-that-are-popular on twitter (blake w cal ai, or jack friks) to invite speculation on their short-term, focused progress. it's a simple yes/no market.

concern

will it get enough liquidity? will it *actually* become a distribution cheat code for startups? imo you just need 1-2 prediction markets to go off. new age startups are a new asset class which haven't found a way to a) bootstrap distribution b) get quality eyeballs c) go viral (speculation can 10x the quickness)
